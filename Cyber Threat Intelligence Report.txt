Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) Report
Date of Access: 09 November 2025
Date of Information: 09 November 2025
Reporting Period: 06 November 2025 - 09 November 2025
Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.
The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant cyberspace developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on vulnerabilities and exploits, ransomware and malware, and cyberattacks and campaigns.
---------------------------------------------
Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant cyber threat activities highlighted the escalating sophistication of state-sponsored actors and the vulnerability of critical infrastructures. A China-linked group targeted a U.S. non-profit organization, establishing long-term access as part of an ongoing espionage campaign against U.S. entities. The Congressional Budget Office confirmed a breach attributed to foreign hackers, exposing sensitive financial research data. Additionally, the Clop ransomware gang successfully compromised The Washington Post via vulnerabilities in Oracle software, demonstrating a broader trend of exploiting software weaknesses. Furthermore, researchers noted an increase in the integration of AI into malware for improved evasion tactics, underscoring an evolution in cybercriminal methodologies.
Vulnerabilities & Exploits: During the reporting period, significant cybersecurity incidents emerged, including the exploitation of a zero-day vulnerability in Samsung Galaxy devices, tracked as CVE-2025-21042, which allowed the deployment of a sophisticated spyware known as LANDFALL in targeted attacks, particularly in the Middle East. Additionally, a critical vulnerability was disclosed in the npm package expr-eval, which posed a risk of arbitrary code execution, while Redis issued a security advisory for CVE-2025-49844, affirming the potential for critical exploitation in applications utilizing Lua scripting. The Congressional Budget Office confirmed a breach attributed to foreign hackers, leading to the exposure of sensitive financial research, prompting immediate containment measures. Overall, these incidents highlight ongoing threats to cybersecurity across various platforms and sectors.
Ransomware & Malware: During the reporting period, a notable cyber espionage incident linked to a China-associated group was reported, targeting a U.S. non-profit organization with the objective of establishing long-term access, as part of an ongoing campaign against U.S. entities engaged in policy matters. Furthermore, vulnerabilities exploited in Samsung devices led to the deployment of previously undocumented spyware called LANDFALL, which operated for nearly a year, emphasizing the increasing sophistication of espionage techniques. Additionally, a rise in fraudulent activities involving malicious applications was documented, with threat actors leveraging outdated systems to target various sectors. Concurrently, the operational landscape showed an escalation in attacks by Russian groups employing advanced techniques to propagate malware through seemingly legitimate software installations. Overall, the findings underscore the evolving tactics employed by sophisticated threat actors and their increasing targeting of critical infrastructure and policy-related organizations.
Cyberattacks & Campaigns: During the reporting period, significant cyber threat activities were observed, particularly involving state-sponsored actors and high-profile breaches. A China-linked group targeted a U.S. policy-oriented non-profit organization, establishing long-term access to their systems as part of broader espionage efforts against U.S. entities. Concurrently, the Congressional Budget Office confirmed a breach possibly linked to foreign hackers, compromising sensitive financial research data. Additionally, the Clop ransomware gang was reported to have compromised The Washington Post through vulnerabilities in Oracle software, highlighting a notable trend in exploiting software weaknesses. Researchers also identified the use of AI in malware to enhance evasion tactics, indicating a shift in cybercriminal methodologies.
