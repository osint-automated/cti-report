Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) Report

Date of Access: 01 November 2025

Date of Information: 01 November 2025

Reporting Period: 25 October 2025 - 01 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant cyberspace developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on vulnerabilities and exploits, ransomware and malware, and cyberattacks and campaigns.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant developments in cybersecurity were marked by increased activity from state-sponsored cyber-espionage groups, particularly those linked to China, such as Bronze Butler and UNC6384, who exploited zero-day vulnerabilities in platforms like Windows and Motex Lanscope Endpoint Manager to conduct targeted operations against European diplomats. The surge in cybercriminal activity was evidenced by North Korea-linked hackers reportedly stealing over $2 billion in cryptocurrency, while the dark web saw the emergence of 300 million stolen credentials and advanced scams employing AI manipulation techniques. Moreover, the U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) responded to escalating threats by updating its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, highlighting a landscape rife with operational challenges as organizations face ongoing risks from unaddressed vulnerabilities, alongside a recent major data breach impacting 10.5 million individuals at Conduent Business Services. To mitigate these threats, a need for enhanced security measures has been underscored across various sectors.

  • Vulnerabilities & Exploits: During the reporting period, significant events and trends in cybersecurity and international relations were observed. Notably, the recent exploitation of a zero-day vulnerability in Motex Lanscope Endpoint Manager by China-linked cyber-espionage actors known as 'Bronze Butler' raises concerns about targeted cyber operations. Additionally, CISA issued a warning regarding ransomware gangs leveraging an older Linux kernel flaw, highlighting the persistent risk posed by known vulnerabilities to Linux systems. The geopolitical landscape was marked by a U.S.-China agreement to de-escalate trade tensions, which included concessions on rare earth exports, relevant for technology and defense sectors facing supply chain challenges. Furthermore, the rising use of AI technologies in browsers and services has introduced new vulnerabilities, with reports of attacks exploiting prompt injection vectors to manipulate responses and compromise user data.
  • Ransomware & Malware: During the reporting period, North Korea-linked hackers reportedly stole over $2 billion in cryptocurrency in 2025, demonstrating a notable increase in cybercriminal activity compared to previous years, while global law enforcement recovered $439 million and apprehended hundreds of money launderers across 40 countries during a four-month operation. Additionally, China's state-sponsored actors have been linked to multiple cyber-espionage campaigns, exploiting various unpatched vulnerabilities, including Windows zero-days to target European diplomats. A surge in cyber threats was also observed in the dark web, with reports of 300 million stolen credentials and advanced scams utilizing malware and prompt injection techniques. Meanwhile, the U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) updated its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, responding to high-severity flaws impacting widely-used software, underlining a challenging landscape for cybersecurity amidst increasing operational risks faced by organizations globally.
  • Cyberattacks & Campaigns: During the reporting period, significant cyber threats and incidents have emerged, particularly from China-linked cyber-espionage groups such as UNC6384 and Bronze Butler, who exploited zero-day vulnerabilities in Windows and Motex Lanscope Endpoint Manager to target European diplomats and execute sophisticated attacks. In addition, data breaches have proliferated, exemplified by the confirmation that over 300 million stolen credentials are being traded on the dark web, and a massive data breach at Conduent Business Services reportedly impacted 10.5 million individuals. Cybersecurity concerns were further highlighted by a recent analysis indicating a notable increase in attacks utilizing AI technologies, prompting calls for enhanced security measures across organizations. The trend of nation-state actors employing advanced persistent threats (APTs) underscores the increasing complexity and urgency of addressing cybersecurity in both public and private sectors.
