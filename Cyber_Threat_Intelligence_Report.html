Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) Report

Date of Access: 06 November 2025

Date of Information: 06 November 2025

Reporting Period: 03 November 2025 - 06 November 2025

Distribution: Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant cyberspace developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on vulnerabilities and exploits, ransomware and malware, and cyberattacks and campaigns.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, several critical cybersecurity incidents underscored the escalating threats facing organizations, including a state-sponsored breach identified by SonicWall linked to firewall configurations, and a significant data theft at the University of Pennsylvania impacting alumni information. The financial ramifications of cyber threats were illustrated by Marks and Spencer's substantial 55.4% drop in profits, attributed to a disruptive cyberattack in April. New vulnerabilities, notably in Microsoft Teams, raised impersonation risks, while the emergence of advanced backdoors such as SesameOp, which leverages OpenAI's API, demonstrated the increasing sophistication of cybercriminal tactics. Furthermore, cybersecurity advisories emphasized password security as a pivotal vulnerability, driving home the necessity for enhanced security measures and proactive threat mitigation strategies across all sectors.

  • Vulnerabilities & Exploits: During the reporting period, a notable resurgence in app popularity was observed with the controversial app Neon, designed to compensate users for phone call recordings utilized in AI training, despite facing previous operational challenges. In cybersecurity, a series of vulnerabilities affecting Cisco's Identity Services Engine and Unified Contact Center products were disclosed, including critical flaws allowing remote attackers to execute commands and cause system disruptions. Additionally, CISA updated its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog, incorporating multiple new critical vulnerabilities affecting various platforms, underlining the ongoing risk of active exploitation. Meanwhile, the cryptocurrency market exhibited volatility with Bitcoin dipping below $100,000 before a minor rebound, and vulnerabilities were identified in popular npm packages and AI systems, spotlighting the increasing sophistication of threats exploiting emerging technologies.

Cybercriminals are increasingly leveraging artificial intelligence tools, including OpenAI's Assistants API for command and control, as well as the emergence of sophisticated malware like PROMPTFLUX, which dynamically generates obfuscation techniques. Moreover, notable vulnerabilities were reported in Microsoft Teams, allowing for serious impersonation risks, while significant exploitations of cloud services and the rise of Living-off-the-Land techniques were also observed, indicating that adversaries are continuously adapting their strategies to exploit new technologies and human weaknesses for financial gain.
  • Cyberattacks & Campaigns: During the reporting period, significant cybersecurity incidents were reported, highlighting ongoing vulnerabilities and the growing sophistication of threat actors. SonicWall concluded that a September security breach was conducted by state-sponsored hackers, while the University of Pennsylvania confirmed a data breach affecting its internal systems, resulting in unauthorized access to alumni data. Marks and Spencer reported a 55.4% decline in first-half profits due to an April cyberattack that severely disrupted their operations, reflecting the substantial financial impact of cybersecurity threats on businesses. Additionally, a new backdoor dubbed SesameOp was discovered by Microsoft, utilizing the OpenAI API for stealthy command-and-control operations, underlining the evolving tactics employed by cybercriminals. Furthermore, weaknesses related to password security were identified as a major risk factor across organizations, emphasizing the critical need for enhanced security measures and threat mitigation strategies.
